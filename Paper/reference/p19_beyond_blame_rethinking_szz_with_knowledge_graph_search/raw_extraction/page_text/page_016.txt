Category-level analysis reveals the strengths of LLM4SZZ vs. AgenticSZZ.Table 6 and
Figure 5 break down the true positives of each approach by BIC category. AgenticSZZ achieves
the highest total number of true positives (1,408) through substantial gains in Blame (+193 over
LLM4SZZ) and Blameless (+57) categories. The Venn diagrams reveal that the two approaches are
largely complementary in the Blame category (998 shared successes, but AgenticSZZ uniquely solves
211 cases vs. LLM4SZZ’s 18), while they solve completely different cases in the Ancestor category (no
overlap). Interestingly, LLM4SZZ’s context expansion finds more Ancestor-category BICs (35 vs. 19),
suggesting that expanding context around modified lines, while aimed to improve the identification
of blameless cases, actually is more efficient in finding Ancestor cases. In contrast, AgenticSZZ’s
superior reasoning enabled it to correctly identify 193 more Blame BICs than LLM4SZZ. This
indicates that intelligent candidate selection matters more than search space expansion for the
majority of cases.
The TKG-agent synergy reflects an exploration-exploitation trade-off [ 33].The TKG
expands the frontier of possible solutions by encoding temporal and code-level relationships,
while the agent exploits semantic understanding to select among them. Neither component alone is
sufficient: expansion without intelligence introduces noise, while intelligence without expansion hits
a coverage ceiling. This complementary design suggests that future work could explore integrating
search-based software engineering techniques [13] to further optimize the navigation strategy.
