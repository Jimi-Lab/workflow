Limitations / Threats / Ethics Analysis

Stated / Implied Limitations:
- The paper is preliminary and states that successful use in countermeasure requirements analysis is needed for further validation.
- The authors acknowledge that the patent corpus has adversarial characteristics and differs from academic peer-reviewed literature.
- They state the source datasets are too large to analyze entirely manually and that automation is required.
- NLP/topic modeling and summarization were tried but found insufficient for creating the desired semantic representation.

Validity Threats:
- Source bias: patents may emphasize novelty and business objectives rather than objective technical fidelity.
- Coverage bias: the initial model depends heavily on selected patents and MITRE-related knowledge bases.
- Curation subjectivity: technique names and abstractions depend on expert judgment.
- Adoption validity: model usefulness depends on practitioner uptake across use cases.
- Maintenance burden: cybersecurity countermeasure technology changes rapidly.

Security/Ethics Considerations:
- A detailed defense knowledge graph can help defenders understand coverage and gaps, but also clarifies defensive capability structure.
- The paper focuses on public/technical corpora and countermeasure semantics; no human-subject or private-data protocol is described. [NEEDS EVIDENCE]
