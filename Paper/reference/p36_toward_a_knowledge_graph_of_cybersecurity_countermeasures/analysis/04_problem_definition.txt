Problem Definition

Target Problem:
- Build a precise, semantically rigorous knowledge graph for cybersecurity countermeasures.
- Enable practitioners to reason about what defensive capabilities do, how they work, what artifacts they interact with, and how they relate to offensive TTPs.

Inputs / Evidence Sources:
- Cybersecurity patents and patent metadata.
- Existing knowledge bases such as MITRE CAR and ATT&CK.
- Academic papers, technical specifications, product documentation, and other public technical corpora.
- The local source tree adds OWL/RDF ontology files, SPARQL queries, mapping spreadsheets, and update scripts as artifact evidence.

Core Modeling Objects:
- Defensive tactics and techniques.
- Digital artifacts and digital objects.
- Countermeasure capabilities.
- Offensive techniques/TTPs.
- Relations such as produces, executes, analyzes, accesses, installs, observes, detects, counters, and generic associated-with style links.

Reasoning Goal:
- Do not manually enumerate every offense-defense pair.
- Represent offensive techniques, defensive techniques, and digital artifacts separately, then infer relationships through shared artifacts and typed relations.

Relation to VulnVersion:
- D3FEND's target is not "given CVE+patch+target version, decide affectedness."
- Its transferable problem abstraction is evidence-mediated reasoning: define typed entities and relations so the agent can infer a judgment from structured evidence rather than from ungrounded text.
