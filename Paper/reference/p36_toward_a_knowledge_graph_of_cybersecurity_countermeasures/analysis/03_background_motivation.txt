Background / Related Work Analysis

Reference Families:
- MITRE standards and formats: CVE, CWE, OVAL, CPE, CEE, CAPEC, MAEC, CybOX.
- Policy/framework sources: NIST Cybersecurity Framework, SP 800-53, DHS/DoDCAR, cyber threat frameworks.
- ATT&CK/CAR: ATT&CK models adversary tactics/techniques, while CAR catalogs analytics and maps them to ATT&CK techniques.
- Cybersecurity ontologies: information security ontology, vulnerability-management ontology, UCO, cyber-security domain ontologies, and linked-data systems.
- General knowledge graph and linked open data work.

Positioning Strategy:
- D3FEND is framed as complementary to ATT&CK: ATT&CK focuses on adversary behavior, D3FEND focuses on countermeasures.
- It distinguishes enumerative vocabularies from semantic, relation-rich, inferential models.
- It argues for a capability-engineering perspective rather than incident-response-only vocabulary.

Useful Taxonomy for VulnVersion:
- Data identifiers: CVE/CWE/CPE/CAPEC.
- Attack behavior models: ATT&CK.
- Defensive/countermeasure models: D3FEND.
- Evidence-object ontologies: Digital Artifact Ontology.
- Knowledge-graph infrastructure: OWL/RDF/SPARQL/linked data.

Non-Transferable Point:
- The background does not cover vulnerability affected-version identification as a target task.
- Use p36 for ontology/attack-defense graph design, not as a direct affected-version baseline.
