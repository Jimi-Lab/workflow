Method / Ontology Design Analysis

Method Overview:
- The paper combines three information-organization approaches: conceptual framework, domain knowledge model, and knowledge graph.
- It builds D3FEND bottom-up from technical sources, especially patents, while refining higher-level abstractions as semantic patterns emerge.

Data Source Method:
- The patent corpus is used because patents contain engineering-level claims, citations, official categories, and novelty assertions.
- Existing MITRE knowledge bases and ATT&CK are analyzed and mapped into D3FEND.
- Additional sources include academic papers, technical specifications, and product technical documentation.

Countermeasure Analysis Process:
- The paper reports early attempts with unsupervised topic modeling and text summarization, then states these were insufficient for semantic representation.
- The adopted approach is manual/curated analysis to identify abstract defensive techniques and digital artifacts, with future automation as the roadmap.
- This is a strong research pattern: start with human-grounded curation to define ontology quality before automating extraction.

D3FEND Model:
- D3FEND includes the knowledge graph, user interface, and knowledge model.
- Defensive tactics are high-level maneuvers, while techniques are methods for implementing those maneuvers.
- D3FEND technique naming is treated as a high-dimensional optimization problem: maximize information, minimize confusion, and prefer computer science / computer engineering semantics where relevant.
- Example technique: process code segment verification, used to demonstrate semantic specificity and evidence grounding.

Digital Artifact Ontology:
- The DAO defines concepts needed to classify and represent digital objects relevant to cybersecurity analysis.
- A digital object becomes a digital artifact when a cyber actor interacts with it.
- Digital artifacts establish conceptual scope: if an item cannot exist in computer or network infrastructure, it is outside the ontology.
- Compound artifacts are supported through hierarchical/compositional representation.

Attack-Defense Mapping:
- Offensive and defensive techniques are associated with digital artifacts through typed relations.
- The key inference pattern is artifact-mediated: accurately model offense-artifact and defense-artifact relations, then infer offense-defense relationships through the shared artifact layer.
- This is directly useful for VulnVersion graph design: affectedness can be modeled through root cause, patch, target code, build artifact, version/tag, and vulnerability-condition nodes rather than direct untyped pairwise labels.

Static Source Evidence:
- README identifies src/ontology/d3fend-protege.ttl as the editable ontology source and make all as the build workflow.
- d3fend-protege.ttl is a large OWL/RDF Turtle ontology with thousands of classes/properties and subclass assertions.
- Makefile uses ROBOT, Jena, SPARQL queries, Blazegraph endpoints, and dist/test targets.
- src/queries contains report and quality-check queries such as missing labels, duplicate labels, missing definitions, inconsistent IRIs, missing ATT&CK IDs, and restriction conversion.
- update scripts support ATT&CK, CAPEC, SPARTA, ATLAS, CCI, NIST, and CWE-related mapping workflows.
