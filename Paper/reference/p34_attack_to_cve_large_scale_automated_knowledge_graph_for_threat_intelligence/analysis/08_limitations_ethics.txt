Limitations, Threats, and Ethics

Method limitations:
- Cross-taxonomy links can imply plausible exploitation paths but do not prove that a specific software version is affected.
- CVE/CWE/CAPEC/ATT&CK links inherit incompleteness and lag from upstream repositories.
- Hierarchical expansion may trade recall for candidate explosion and false positives; bounded expansion needs justification.
- CTI graph construction is not the same as code-level patch containment.

Artifact limitations:
- Local artifact is documentation/CSV/images only; implementation source for the paper's automated pipeline was not detected. [NEEDS ARTIFACT]
- PDF table/figure extraction is incomplete. [NEEDS TABLE REPAIR] [NEEDS FIGURE EXTRACTION]
- Citation metadata needs verification. [NEEDS CITATION VERIFICATION]

Ethics/security:
- The work is defensive CTI integration, but mappings from weaknesses to attack techniques can also aid adversarial planning. In VulnVersion writing, emphasize patch prioritization, exposure reduction, and defensive triage.
