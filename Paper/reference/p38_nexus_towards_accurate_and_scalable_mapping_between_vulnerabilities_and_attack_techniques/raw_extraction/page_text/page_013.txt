B. Case Study II: Ambiguous Descriptions and Missing Links
This case study (Figure 23) shows howNEXUS handles am-
biguous CVE descriptions and restores important TTPs [23]–
[26] missing from default CVE-to-TTP links [45]–[48]. The
chosen CVE, CVE-2025-61882 (Base Score 9.8 / Critical),
contains vague, non-actionable text (“Vulnerability in the Or-
acle . . . ”,“CVSS 3.1 Base Score . . . ”) and no clear indication
of ﬂaw type or exploitation steps (“Easily exploitable . . . ”,
“attacker with network access via HTTP . . . ”). To address
this ambiguity, NEXUS applies the attack-pattern extraction
step from Section III-C, discarding irrelevant phrases (red
in Figure 23) and retaining only clauses with actual attack
semantics (green).
Next, to predict and reﬁne TTPs, NEXUS applies the
steps in Section III-F to the green-highlighted clauses. From
these, it infers network-centric TTPs such as T1102 (Web
Service) and T1071.001 (Web Protocols), along with plau-
sible follow-on behaviors like T1518 (Software Discov-
ery), T1020 (Automated Exﬁltration), and T1489 (Service
Stop), reﬂecting potential post-compromise actions implied
in the text. We then compare these predictions with de-
fault CVE–CWE–CAPEC–TTP mappings: green entries (e.g.,
T1505.003) mark existing public linkages, while red entries
show additional TTPs discovered byNEXUS.
