# 11 Relevance to Our Paper

High-Value Reuse:
- V-SZZ is directly relevant as an affected-version identification baseline.
- It offers a strong motivation for why affected-version metadata cannot be trusted blindly.
- It provides terminology and task structure around vulnerability-inducing commits, fixing commits, duplicated changes, version tags, and affected versions.

Method Contrast:
- V-SZZ is a deterministic, line-history and SZZ-based approach.
- It assumes known fixing commits and tracks vulnerable lines backward.
- If our system uses agents, memory, runtime verification, or VulnTree-style reasoning, V-SZZ can serve as a baseline that lacks semantic agent planning and verifier-gated multi-stage reasoning.

Evaluation Reuse:
- The paper separates VIC identification from affected-version refinement; this separation is useful for our evaluation design.
- The false-positive/false-negative discussion can support why affected-version tools need conservative but evidence-backed verification.
- Manual boundary-version checking provides a gold-standard style, but it is expensive and not scalable.

Citation Use Cases:
- Use in Introduction for NVD/CVE affected-version unreliability and SCA/remediation cost.
- Use in Background for SZZ variants and vulnerability-specific SZZ limitations.
- Use in Related Work as SZZ-based affected-version refinement.
- Use in Experiments as a baseline or baseline family if our pipeline targets affected versions.
