rithms, we remove the Remove and Add Mapping Ghost cases
from KFC dataset. The experimental results for this modiﬁed
dataset are presented in Table VI, which should be compared
to the original results in Table II. “Filter RMG” refers to the re-
moval of Remove Mapping Ghost bug-ﬁxing commits from our
dataset. “Filter AMG” indicates the removal of Add Mapping
Ghost bug-inducing commits from our dataset. “Filter RMG,
AMG” signiﬁes the removal of both types of commits from
our dataset. For example, when considering the B-SZZ algo-
rithm, as shown in Table VI, only ﬁltering Add Mapping Ghost
reduces the recall from 0.71 to 0.58 (↓22.4%), compared to
ﬁltering both Remove and Add Mapping Ghost. While ﬁltering
only Remove Mapping Ghost leads to a decrease in recall to 0.7
(↓1.4%). These ﬁndings suggest that Remove Mapping Ghost
has a more pronounced negative impact on the recall of SZZ al-
gorithms. When compared to the results in Table II, the average
